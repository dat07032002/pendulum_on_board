## 2. Success-metric correction

The old metric credited a policy after a short successful catch, even if it subsequently fell.
That made unstable policies look solved.

`rl/furuta_env.py` now distinguishes:

- `is_catch_success`: the policy reached the balance condition at some point.
- `is_success`: the episode ended normally and the policy occupied the balance condition for at
  least 80% of the final two seconds (400 control ticks at 200 Hz).

`rl/eval_policy.py` reports both metrics over fresh deterministic-policy episodes. Use sustained
success for model selection. Always verify a claimed winner with at least 500 fresh episodes and
the same `--seed0`; 100-episode peaks have repeatedly been sampling noise.

This correction changed the interpretation of the clean-tilt master:

- Earlier/easier metric: approximately 0.95 at clean ±20° tilt.
- Corrected sustained metric: approximately **0.71** at clean ±20° tilt.

The skill still exists, but its true reliability is lower than the earlier handoff claimed.

## 3. Phase C attempts before retention-aware fine-tuning

All attempts warm-started from the clean-tilt model unless stated otherwise.

| Attempt | Main change | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| v1 | TQC, `tqd=2`, actor/critic LR `1e-4` | Training eval climbed to 0.53, then collapsed. |
| v2 | More pessimistic critic, `tqd=3`, LR `1e-4` | Worse: roughly 0.23 → 0; pessimism did not cure the failure. |
| v3 | Gentler `tqd=2`, LR `3e-5` path | Preserved as `phasec_v3_verified63.zip`; independently verified at **39.8% sustained** on 500 full-DR ±20° episodes. |
| v4/v5 | Further DR-ramp/curriculum variants | Did not beat v3 and were stopped. |

Diagnosis: naive off-policy fine-tuning under a distribution shift destroys or destabilizes the
pretrained behavior. The main problem is forgetting/retention, not simply TQC overestimation.

The design work was informed by:

- *Fine-tuning Reinforcement Learning Models is Secretly a Forgetting Mitigation Problem*
  (ICML 2024).
- *Experience Replay for Continual Learning* / CLEAR (NeurIPS 2019).
- *Offline-to-Online Reinforcement Learning via Balanced Replay and Pessimistic Q-Ensemble*
  (CoRL 2022).
- *Efficient Online Reinforcement Learning with Offline Data* / RLPD (ICML 2023).

## 4. Retention-aware implementation

New files:

- `rl/retention_tqc.py`
  - subclasses TQC while retaining the installed TQC update structure;
  - mixes fixed teacher data and new online data in every update;
  - supports separate actor and critic learning rates;
  - freezes the actor during critic adaptation;
  - adds behavior-cloning MSE against teacher actions;
  - retains complete model and optimizer state when resuming.
- `rl/collect_teacher_data.py`
  - collects only successful clean ±20° free-arm episodes;
  - stores observations, teacher actions, next observations, rewards, and bootstrap-safe done flags.

The server teacher dataset is `rl/teacher_clean20_200k.npz`:

- 200,000 transitions;
- 100 accepted successful episodes;
- 142 attempted episodes.

Default Pilot C retention schedule:

- steps 0–50k: collect online data, no updates;
- 50k–100k: critic-only adaptation;
- 100k–200k: actor enabled at LR `1e-6`;
- critic LR `3e-5`;
- replay batch: 50% successful teacher data, 50% online data;
- teacher behavior coefficient: 100;
- clean sustained-success guard: stop below 0.60.

`rl/train_tqc.py` also now:

- saves `best_success_model.zip` separately from SB3's mean-reward `best_model.zip`;
- evaluates clean retention during retention runs;
- supports DR probability and range ramps;
- exposes `--p_corner`;
- validates `p_corner` in [0, 1];
- logs sustained and catch success fields.

## 5. Retention Pilot C result

One 200k seed was run from `tilt_nodr_s0/best_model.zip`, initially at clean stage 4.

- Clean sustained success stayed near 69–78%, so catastrophic forgetting was prevented.
- The best 100-episode training eval appeared to be 41% at 80k.
- Independent verification on 500 fresh episodes showed only:
  - sustained: **26.4% (132/500)**
  - catch: **46%**
- On the identical 500 seeds, v3 achieved:
  - sustained: **39.8% (199/500)**
  - catch: **59%**

Conclusion: replay plus behavior retention solved the forgetting symptom, but that pilot did not
improve robust performance. Its 41% training-eval peak was noise. It also exposed an experimental
design issue: a 200k run starting at stage 4 may never enter plant DR under the corrected gate.

## 6. Forced-corner ablation

Full plant DR samples these values once per active episode:

| Parameter | Full range |
|---|---:|
| motor gear | 0.010–0.016 |
| arm damping | 0.0003–0.0010 |
| pole damping | 0.00002–0.00010 |
| arm friction | 0.004–0.008 |
| pole friction | 0.0002–0.0006 |
| pole inertia | 92–108% of nominal |
| encoder observation noise | 0–0.01 |
| action delay | 1–3 control steps |
| IMU tilt noise | σ = 0.005 rad (about 0.29°) |

Previously, every draw had a 10% chance of being forced exactly to a minimum or maximum
(`p_corner=0.10`). The ablation disabled that atom at the endpoints while retaining uniform
sampling across the complete ranges (`--p_corner 0`).

Three 200k retention runs used the same clean warm-start and separate seeds/GPUs. The first
launches were caught entering clean stage 4 and stopped before meaningful updates; the corrected
runs started directly at stage 9:

- 90% of training episodes: full-range DR, no forced endpoints;
- 10%: clean rehearsal;
- full ±20° tilt;
- the retention schedule from §4.

Results from the 100-episode in-training evaluator:

| Seed | Best step | Best DR sustained | Clean at that point | Final DR | Final clean |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | 120k | 39% | 73% | 24% | 73% |
| 1 | 160k | 42% | 77% | 33% | 79% |
| 2 | 180k | **47%** | **78%** | 37% | 66% |

All runs completed their fixed 200k budget normally. None reached the 0.80 robust early-stop and
none crossed the 0.60 clean-retention floor. The final policies were worse than their peaks, so
use `best_success_model.zip`, not `tqc_final.zip`.

These results are promising but **not verified**. The previous Pilot C also produced a misleading
100-episode peak. Do not declare no-corner seed 2 better than v3 until the next step is complete.

to cause the robustness loss.
